The Compliance Chasm for SMB Healthcare

The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act legalizes consumer privacy, forcing every diagnostic lab, local clinic, and medical facility to step up as a regulated Data Fiduciary.

Traditional fast-paced actions—such as routing lab updates or image scans via legacy consumer messaging software or unencrypted email chains—carry an existential risk profile: immediate regulatory penalties capping at ₹250 Crore for data mishandling.

DPDP Section 7: Unambiguous Consent

The Interaction: Two Taps to Verification

DPDP states that consent must be explicit, conditional, specific, and revocable. It cannot be buried inside standard end-user license fine print.

The patient simply brings up their secure Mobile Health Wallet application and scans the provider's screen. The interface strips down complex legalese into clear, simple options. By authorizing the prompt, verifiable explicit consent is generated natively without creating real-world friction inside the laboratory reception workspace.

DPDP Section 11-14: Rights of Principal

The Guardian Layer: Native Privacy Protection

Traditional architecture stores identity tokens and file pathways on single centralized servers, establishing prime hacking targets. MedProof removes this threat architecture entirely by running a silent cryptographic trust backend.

The system decouples the entire operational workflow across dedicated infrastructure nodes: identity matching travels through IOMe, encrypted information slices reside inside Kapsul, and lifecycle access controls are managed by MOI Blockchain smart contracts. Seamless peer-to-peer transmission is coordinated exclusively by Treo, ensuring records travel safely from the facility directly to the app without lingering on temporary servers.

DPDP Section 8(6): Breach Notification

Automated Security Ledger & Incident Tracking

Data Fiduciaries are statutorily required to notify the Data Protection Board and every affected citizen instantly if an operational anomaly or breach signature occurs.

MedProof implements an immutable Incident Logging Engine right into the core architecture ledger. Every modification, expired token window, data access query, or authorization change is permanently captured. If an unauthorized infrastructure access query trips the smart contract rules, the ledger automatically locks down data streams and prints an auditable security trail instantly ready for compliance review.
